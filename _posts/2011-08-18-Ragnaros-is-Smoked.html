Now, World of Warcraft isn’t well known for its miracles. There have been none that I’ve seen in 7 years, until last night where I can only say is the first time I have seen something happen that really shouldn’t have.

We were on 15%, and people started dying, but we pressed on to try and push the kill. Slowly, each one of us dropped dead from [insert excuse why you would still be standing in fire]. The third phase is pretty damn easy – avoid smash, waves, engulfing flames and meteors. So long as you are not blind, you can generally avoid everything. Alas, we were dropping dead like flies locked in a greenhouse on a very hot day.

Last thing I remember doing was watching the raid frames and Ragnaros’ health frame, seeing only DPS remained alive with the boss on 13.5%.

We knew Ragnaros needed to be at 10% for the kill, so it was 3.5% to go. Then I saw 12%; then quickly 11.5%, with only Leatunell alive, still dodging the fire like the boss he claims he is. Within a quick heartbeat, Leatunell fell at 10.5%. It was a wipe; suddenly:

What the hell?

Nerdgasms on Mumble, screams of joy and disbelief. What was this? The only explanation we can find is that the travel time on Leatunell’s shots kept Ragnaros in combat after we had wiped, and it just so happened that we only needed 10.5% for the kill, with these final shots netting it.

Is it a miracle? No. It fucking isn’t, because miracles don’t exist. It was, however, damn lucky, and I still think it wasn’t deserved. But screw it; loot!

Why so many Ogres?
